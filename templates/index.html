About This Project

Understanding the problem, objectives, and clinical relevance of early retinal screening

Problem Statement

Myopia (nearsightedness) is one of the most common refractive errors worldwide. In severe cases, Pathological Myopia can lead to progressive retinal degeneration and vision loss. Early identification through fundus image analysis is crucial for preventing complications.

Project Objective

To develop an AI-based system that classifies fundus retinal images into Normal, Myopia, and Pathological Myopia categories using deep learning techniques, providing confidence scores and structured clinical recommendations.

Clinical Importance

Pathological Myopia increases the risk of retinal detachment, macular degeneration, and permanent vision impairment. Automated screening tools enable early risk assessment and reduce dependency on manual evaluation.

Scope: This system focuses exclusively on detection of Myopia and Pathological Myopia from fundus retinal images.

How It Works

The diagnostic pipeline from image upload to clinical recommendation

1

Upload Image

Clinician or patient uploads a fundus retinal photograph in standard format (JPEG/PNG)

2

Deep CNN model predicts myopia condition

EfficientNet-B3 deep CNN model classifies the image into one of the defined retinal disease categories

3

GradCAM Visualization

GradCAM visualization highlights retinal regions influencing the AI prediction decision

4

Confidence Score

Probabilistic confidence values are computed for each predicted class using softmax output

5

Clinical Report

Structured clinical recommendation and severity grading is generated for the reviewing clinician

System Features

Key capabilities of the AI-Based Retinal Disease Detection platform

Role-Based Access Control

Separate portals for Doctors and Patients with differentiated permissions, dashboards, and data visibility scoped to role requirements.

AI-Powered Diagnosis

EfficientNet-B3-based multi-class classification trained on ophthalmic datasets for reliable automated identification of retinal pathologies.

GradCAM Visualization

GradCAM visualization highlights diagnostically important retinal regions boundary delineation with color-coded overlay maps for visual interpretation.

Dashboard Analytics

Clinical dashboards with case statistics, diagnosis distribution charts, and longitudinal patient history tracking for monitoring trends.

Clinical Decision Support

Structured diagnostic reports with severity grading, recommended clinical actions, and referral suggestions based on AI findings.

Secure Patient Data

Protected patient records with authenticated access, audit logging, and compliance-oriented data handling for medical privacy.
